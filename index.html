What is SAMA?

A regional alliance of independent, founder-led agencies united to deliver hyperlocal marketing expertise across Southeast Asia without the constraints of global networks.

Beyond referrals only — we're building a collaborative ecosystem that combines relationships, business opportunities, market access, and industry positioning.

Our Mission

To champion borderless marketing in APAC by uniting the region's boldest independent agencies, and by empowering them with the tools, talents, and partnerships to deliver business and marketing solutions that are hyper-local, challenge conventions, and push marketing boundaries.

What This Means

🎯 Leadership Commitment

SAMA is built by founders and senior decision-makers who are willing to help shape the alliance collectively. Leadership is reflected through initiative, contribution, and participation—not just formal titles.

You are expected to:
  • Take ownership of initiatives, projects, or working groups
  • Support Country or Regional Committee responsibilities
  • Participate in discussions around strategy, recruitment, partnerships, and ecosystem growth
  • Contribute ideas, introductions, market opportunities, or industry insights
  • Represent SAMA professionally within your local market and externally
  • Help guide newer members and strengthen cross-country collaboration

✦ SAMA operates on a peer-to-peer leadership model where members are expected to contribute actively rather than participate passively.

🤝 Active Engagement & Participation

The alliance operates on a strong give-and-take culture where value is closely tied to participation, responsiveness, and contribution. Members are expected to remain consistently engaged in both country and regional activities.

This includes:
  • Attending meetings, gatherings, workshops, and alliance activities consistently
  • Participating beyond commercial transactions alone
  • Introducing opportunities openly and collaborating across markets
  • Sharing market knowledge, agency experiences, tools, and learnings
  • Responding professionally and promptly to member requests or opportunities
  • Supporting alliance logistics, initiatives, or task forces where needed
  • Maintaining regular visibility and communication within the ecosystem

📌 Important:

Appoint a secondary representative or senior team member to ensure continuity of participation when founders are unavailable.

✦ The alliance works best when members actively contribute to conversations, initiatives, and relationship building—not just when business opportunities arise.

💙 Community & Relationship Building

SAMA places strong emphasis on building genuine long-term relationships between founders and agencies across the region. Trust, credibility, and familiarity are core to how members collaborate and refer opportunities confidently.

You are encouraged to:
  • Build relationships before expecting business opportunities
  • Participate openly and collaboratively with other members
  • Support fellow members across different markets and specialties
  • Contribute positively to alliance culture and trust
  • Foster long-term partnerships beyond individual projects

The alliance is designed to create a trusted regional ecosystem where members can rely on one another professionally and personally over time.

⭐ Quality & Professional Standards

SAMA is built on trust and referrals. Maintaining strong professional standards across the alliance is critical. The alliance maintains a selective membership approach to preserve credibility, compatibility, and long-term effectiveness.

You are expected to:
  • Maintain high quality work and professional conduct
  • Uphold the reputation and credibility of the alliance
  • Be transparent, reliable, and collaborative in engagements
  • Respect alliance governance, processes, and member relationships
  • Participate constructively and professionally across all interactions

✦ The alliance prioritises long-term ecosystem quality over rapid expansion or transactional growth.

💰 Financial Commitment

Members are expected to fulfil agreed membership and commercial obligations that support the sustainability and operations of SAMA within their respective country chapter. Membership funds are managed at the country chapter level and support local chapter operations, activities, gatherings, ecosystem initiatives, and country-level development.

Membership Fees:

PRE-COUNTRY LAUNCH

USD 1,000 (one-off)

Contributes towards initial setup, onboarding, country-building efforts, and early ecosystem development.

POST-COUNTRY LAUNCH

USD 1,000 (annual)

Supports ongoing chapter activities, member engagement, operations, administration, and local ecosystem initiatives.

Additional Expenses:

CHAPTER ACTIVITY FEES

Estimated USD 250/year

May be introduced or adjusted by individual country chapters depending on local activities, workshops, networking sessions, events, or operational requirements.

SELF-FUNDED / INCIDENTAL EXPENSES

Estimated USD 1,000/year

Members should anticipate additional discretionary expenses: travel, accommodation, hosted dinners, regional meetings, conference attendance, business development trips, sponsorship contributions, or collaborative initiatives.

✦ Why it matters: Financial commitment ensures country chapters can continue operating meaningful activities, collaborations, member engagement initiatives, and long-term ecosystem development across APAC.
